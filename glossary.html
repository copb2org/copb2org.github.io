Glossary of Common Terms

A quick reference for words you’ll hear at appointments or read about COPB2: genetics and therapy terms in plain language.

Gene

A section of DNA that carries instructions for building and running the body. COPB2 is one gene.

Variant / Mutation

A change in a gene. Some are harmless; some affect health.

Pathogenic

A variant known to cause or contribute to a condition.

Variant of Uncertain Significance (VUS)

A change whose effect isn’t yet fully understood, common for very rare variants.

Carrier

Someone with one altered gene copy who is usually unaffected (relevant to recessive conditions like MCPH19).

De Novo

A brand-new gene change that appears in a child but isn’t carried by either parent.

Autosomal Recessive

Two altered copies (one from each parent) are needed, the pattern for MCPH19.

Autosomal Dominant

One altered copy is enough to cause the condition, the pattern for OPDD.

Hypomorphic

A change that reduces a gene’s function without switching it off completely.

Haploinsufficiency

When one working copy of a gene isn’t enough to keep things fully normal.

Microcephaly

A head (and usually brain) that is significantly smaller than expected for age and sex.

Coatomer (COPI)

The protein “coat” that COPB2 helps build, used to move cargo inside cells.

Spasticity

Increased muscle tone (tightness) that can affect movement; often supported with physiotherapy.

Contracture

A permanent tightening/shortening of muscle or tissue that limits movement; therapy aims to help prevent it.

Orthosis (e.g. AFO)

An external support such as an ankle-foot orthosis, used to position a joint and reduce muscle tightness.

Early Intervention

Therapy and support services for very young children with delays, to make the most of early brain development.
